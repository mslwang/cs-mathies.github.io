From LEARN:

The MATH 137 final exam is coming up on Thursday, December 12th at 9:00am.

No aids allowed (no calculators).  Also, no formula sheet will be provided.
COVERAGE:
The final covers everything from assignments/quizzes 1-10, Assignment MT, and Assignment on Ch. 5.  This includes chapters 1 - 5 of the course notes.
NOTE that section 1.5 (Series), section 3.14 (related rates) and section 5.3 (Big O) are NOT covered on the final.  You will not be asked to classify a discontinuity (section 2.8.1), but you may be asked about continuity/discontinuity.  You will not be asked to compute a limit using Taylor's Approximation Theorem 1 explicitly, though you may use it if you wish.

You do not need to memorize proofs of the major theorems for the exam.  You may need to STATE and USE a theorem/definition from the course notes, but not prove it.  The exam does contain other proofs though.

PRACTICE:
The best things to study are the assignments, the quizzes, the practice midterm, actual midterm, and practice final exams.  Most problems on your final are similar (and in some cases, even the same) as questions that appear on these.  Solutions to the practice exams are coming on Friday.
The Practice Final will have the same instructions as your final and the multiple choice/true false are set up the same way as the quizzes, with the last page containing a bubble sheet you must fill out.

BREAKDOWN:
The exam is 150 minutes long (2.5 hours), and approximately the length of 5 quizzes.  There are:
5 multiple choice questions (material from all parts of the course)
5 True/False questions (material from all parts of the course)
5 short answer questions (material from all parts of the course)
13 long answer questions (some with more than one part):
Questions 1 - 3: Pre-midterm content (Chapters 1, 2).
Questions 4 - 6: Chapter 3 material (Derivatives).
Question 7 - 10: Chapter 4 material (MVT and its applications).
Question 11 & 12: Section 5.1 and 5.2 material (Taylor polynomials and Taylor's Theorem).
Question 13: Curve Sketching (it follows the same template that has been posted).
