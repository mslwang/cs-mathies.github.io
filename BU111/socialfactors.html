Social Factors

PEST

Elements: customs, values, attitudes, demographic characteristics

Significance: affect customer preferences, worker attitudes and behaviors, standards of business conduct, and CSR

Ethics & Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)

personal ethics (individual standards/beliefs regarding what is right/wrong or good/bad); connections: CSF, gaining employee commitment -

managerial ethics (standards or behaviours that guide individual managers in their work); connections: Diamond-E Managerial Preferences, CSF: gaining employee equipment

corporate social responsibility (what organization does to and for stakeholders and how business balances conflicting stakeholder interests); connections: Diamond-E, Strategy

Four levels of CSR

    • procative: looks for opportunities -> exceed the legal expectations willingly and without being asked, Example: HP and their sustainibility initiatives
      accomodative: going above and beyond expectations only when you are asked
      defensive: only meeting legal requirements
      obstructionist: looking for loopholes and shortcuts on legal requirements

  • Importance of CSR

    • making environment manageable - Examples: avoid adverse actions, increase stakeholder support (Tom and their social initiatives - for every shoe purchased they donated one), promote favourable legislations - Examples: develop a sustainibile technology where government requires all businesses to follow suit that will give competitive advantage and reduce rivalry (Porter's)
      meet CSF's - Examples: reducing waste in production process can reduce expenses which increases profitibility, having a proactive stance on CSR ecourages innnovation

    • Stakeholders & CSR

    Stakeholders for social responsibility: natural environment, society, customers, employees (managers), investors

    How to influence managerial preferencs (ethics) in CSR?

    • select: match your hiring criteria with your corporate values
      show: use examplar leadership to represent corporate values
      tell: explicitly state corporate values using mission statements or codes of conduct
      teach: using ethics, booklets and training to teach employees
      reward: set goals and evaluation criteria and give out rewards
      reinforce: develop mechanisms to reinforce values

    • Natural Environment

    Areas to focus CSR: air, water, pollution

    Why we care?

    • paradigm shift in management thinking
      change in societal attitudes - example: 20 years ago attitude was take, make and waste -> today reduce, reuse and recycle
      quantification of impact - example: quantifying the impact of climate change

    • Actions: increase corporate greening not greenwashing (process of conveying a false impression or misleading information about how a company is environmentally friendly)

    Society

    Areas to focus CSR: poverty, health, education

    Why we care?

    • diffuse agents of social decay: political chaos, terrorism
      affect human capabilities
      societal attitudes shift

    • Actions: social entrepreneurship (example: SOS), business and community partnership (example: Danone), develop innovative business models and leverage skills to contribrute to social development (example: Caldwell banker - real estate company that involved their employees in CSR projects)

    Customers

    Areas to focus CSR: pricing, advertising, rights

    Why we care?

    • avoiding adverse actions
      they are the revenue source
      avoid increase regulations

    • Actions: safe products, more information, voice customer concerns, choose, courtesy, education, fair pricing, ethics in advertising and brands

    Employees

    Areas to focus CSR: hiring, promotions, compensation, traning

    Why we care?

    • provide businesses with talent, skills, and labour

    • Actions: responsible hiring and promotions, safe work conditions, provide opportunities for advancement, respectful treatment, protection of whistleblowers

    Investors

    Areas to focus CSR: financial management and reporting

    Why we care?

    • investors provide capital
      neglecting responsibility to investors will cause harsher regulations (may cause legal actions against company)

    • Actions: focus on long-term ROI, responsible management (no insider trading - giving employees more information than the public about your stocks) and transparency in records
