Economic Factors

The Economic Environment

The economic environment refers to the conditions of the economic system in which an organization operates.

Four Pillars of Canadian Financial System

Financial institutions facilitate the flow of money. The four distinct legal areas of the Canadian Financial System are (although the lines between these four pillars have blurred):

  1. Chartered Banks: Make deposits and borrow money + primary lending source for Small and Medium enterprises
    • Implications: deeper insights into customer needs, predictive capabilities anticipate customer needs, foundation of AI
    • Examples:
      • A chartered bank is a privately owned, profit-seeking financial intermediary that serves individuals, businesses, and non-business organization.
      • Chartered banks are the largest and most important financial institutions in Canada. Chartered banks offer chequing and saving accounts, make loans, and provide many services to their customers.
      • They are the main source of short-term loans for business firms.
      • The five largest Canadian banks account for about 90 percent of total bank assets.
      • The Bank Act limits foreign-controlled banks to deposits that do not exceed 8 percent of the total domestic assets of all banks in Canada.
      • The banking business is highly competitive; therefore, banks no longer just accept deposits and make loans.
      • Most banks now offer pension services, trust services, international services, financial advice, and electronic money transfer.
  2. Alternate Banks: Make deposits and borrow money + primary lending source for Small and Medium enterprises
    • Trust Companies: Safeguards funds and estates entrusted to it; may also serve as a trustee, transfer agent, and registrar for corporation
    • Credit Unions: Cooperative savings and lending association formed by a group with common interests.
  3. Specialized Lending/Saving Intermediaries: Used by medium to large businesses + private equity financing/ borrowing
    • Life Insurance Companies: A mutual or stock company that shares risk with its policyholders for payment of premiums.
    • Venture Capital Firms: Specializes in financing installment purchases made by individuals or firms.
    • Pension Funds: Accumulates money that will be paid out to plan subscribers in the future.
  4. Investment Dealers: Used by large and established businesses + going public + stocks and bonds
    • Investment dealers are the primary distributors of new stock and bond issues. They also facilitate secondary trading of stocks and bonds, for example the Toronto Stock Exchange.
