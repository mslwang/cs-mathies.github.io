Chapter 3; Demand, Supply, and Price

Definitions:

quantity demanded: amount of a good or service that consumers want to purchase

demand: relationship between the quantity of a product that buyers want to purchase and the price of that product

substitutes: goods that can be used in place of another good to satisfy similar needs or desires

complements: goods that tend to be consumed together

quantity supplied: amount of a good or service that producers want to sell

supply: relationship between the quantity of some good or service that producers wiah to sell and the price of that product

market: any situation in which buyers and sellers can negotiate the exchange of goods or services

equilibrium price: price at which quantity demanded equals quantity supplied

Chapter 4: Elasticity

Definitions:

inelastic demand: percentage change in quantity demanded is less than percentage change in price; elasticity is less than 1

elastic demand: percentage change in quantity demanded is greater than percentage change in price; elasticity is greater than 1

price elasticity of supply: measure of the responsiveness of quantity supplied to a change in the product's own price

exise tax: tax on the sale of a particular product

tax incidence: location of the burden of a tax - that is, the indentity of the ultimate bearer of the tax (consumer vs. supplier)

income elasticity of demand: measure of responsiveness of quantity demanded to change in income

normal good: good for which quantity demanded rises as income rises - its income elasticity is positive

inferior good: good for which quantity demanded falls as income rises - its income elasticity is negative

necessities: products for which income elasticity of demand is positive but less than one

luxuries: products for which the income elasticity of demand is positive and greater than one

cross elasticity of demand: a measure of responsiveness of the quantity of one product demanded to changes in the price of another product
