Chapter 6: Consumer Behaviour

Definitions:

utility: satisfaction that a consumer receives from consuming some good or service

total utility: consumer's total satisfaction resulting from the consumption of a given product

marginal utility: additional satisfaction obtained from consuming one additional unit of a product

substitution effect: change in the quantity of a product demanded resulting from a change in its relative price

income effect: change in the quantity of a product demanded resulting from a change in real income

Giffen good: an inferior good for which the income effect outweighs the subsititution effect so that the demand curve is positively sloped

consumer surplus: difference between the total value that consumers place

Chapter 7: Producers in the Short Run

Definitions:

single proprietorship: firm that has one owner who is personally responsible for the firm's actions and debts

ordinary partnership: firm that has two or more joint owners, each of whom is personally responsible for the firm's actions and debts

limited partership: firm that has two classes of owners: general partner who take part in managing the firm and are personally liable for the firm's actions and debts, and limited partners, who take no part in the management of the for, and risk only the money they have invested

corporation: firm that has a legal existence seperate from that of the owners

state-owned enterprise: firm that is owned by the government

non-profit organizations: provide goods and services with the objective of just covering their costs (non-governmental organizations)

multinational enterprises: firms that have operations in more than one country

dividends: profits paid out to shareholders of corporation

bond: debt instrument carrying a specified amount, a schedule of interest payments, and (usually) a date for redemption of its face value

production function: functional relation showing
